# Plan — folio on folio

## Palette: specimen-default

The shared `assets/styles.css` cyan/violet on `--ink` deep navy. Used here intentionally because the subject is folio's chrome and patterns, not a single palette. For deck-makers who use folio in earnest, pick a palette from `library/palettes.json` instead.

**Wrong colour budget**: this palette doesn't ship with a stated wrong colour. Acceptable for the meta-example only.

## Sequence

| # | Pattern | Job | Carries |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Manifesto | Open. State the anchor. | "Decks that say one thing well." |
| 02 | Loop | Show the four-phase rhythm | Brief → Plan → Typeset → Bind, looping back |
| 03 | Versus | Contrast slop vs sourced | Generic LLM deck on the left, folio deck on the right |
| 04 | Field Note | Ground with a voice | A quote about specificity from the margin |
| 05 | Surface | The portfolio at a glance | The six palettes as bento tiles |
| 06 | Arc | Close. Restate the anchor. | The journey from sketch to surface; "go pick your sources" |

## Patterns deliberately not used

- **Phase Space** — would need a real journey-over-time dataset; meta-example doesn't have one
- **Cliffhanger** — only 6 slides; an act break would feel forced
- **Stack** — folio doesn't have an architectural stack story to tell
- **Quadrant** — would require positioning folio against competitors; out of scope

If this were a 10-slide deck, Phase + Stack + Quadrant would slot in between Field Note and Surface.
