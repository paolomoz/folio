# Plan · The Water Cycle

*Phase 2 of 4 · folio*

## Palette pick

### Chosen: **Shoji** (`shoji-monastic`, 1640)

Source: Japanese minka interior · winter light through rice paper.
Register: *monastic · domestic · still.*

**Why this one fits**

The brief asked for *deep blue, white, sand/earth accent.* Shoji is that triad already named, and named for places — not for a colour wheel:

| brief asked for | Shoji token | role |
|---|---|---|
| white | `shoji` `#f2eadc` | rice-paper ground |
| sand/earth | `tatami` `#bca77d` | woven straw — secondary surface |
| deep blue | `ai` `#1d3a4e` | indigo cloth, single deep field |
| (structure) | `timber` `#3b2a1d` | stained beam — structural type |
| (body) | `sumi` `#191613` | ink — brushstroke body |
| **wrong colour** | `shu` `#a8554a` | vermilion seal — once per deck |

The register matches the tone we set in the brief: *quietly awed*, not *celebratory*. Shoji's "negative space is itself a colour" rule is exactly how a water-cycle deck should breathe — empty paper standing in for sky, ocean, distance.

### Why not the others

- **Telex** (1986 Olivetti) — civic, paper-and-ink, but no deep blue. Would force us to fake it. Refused.
- **Casio F-91W** — indifferent, slightly sad LCD greens. Wrong register; the cycle is awe, not resignation.
- **Riso Bisque** — federal blue is there, but the register is *scrappy · fluorescent · DIY*. Water-cycle pedagogy is the opposite of zine energy.
- **Night Tube** — midnight blue is tempting, but the register is *nocturnal · slightly tired*. Oceans aren't tired. Streetlamp orange would also dominate every slide.
- **Kodachrome** — cobalt is the right blue but the palette is *vivid · cinematic · hyperreal*. Five saturated colours fighting for the eye while we ask for stillness. Refused.

### Rules we will honour

1. `shu` (vermilion) appears **once in the deck** — as the seal on slide 5, marking the human-impact note.
2. `ai` is **a wall, never a line** — used as deep field for the sky band on slide 3 and the ocean field on slide 2. Never as a 1px rule.
3. `sumi` is body type. `timber` is structural (eyebrows, headers). They do not mix in the same block.
4. **Negative space is a colour.** Shoji ground gets to breathe — at least 40% of every slide is empty paper.
5. No gradients. No drop shadows. (Per palette spec.)

## Pattern map · 5 slides

The water cycle is itself a **Loop**. Rather than spend slide 1 explaining that and then drilling in, we let the structure of the deck embody the loop: open with the anchor, walk three stations as distinct shapes, close with the loop made explicit.

| # | Pattern | Job | Why this pattern |
|---|---------|-----|------------------|
| 1 | **Manifesto** | State the anchor cold: same water, ~4 billion years. | Single bold typographic moment. Lets the audience settle before we move. |
| 2 | **Stack** | Evaporation & transpiration as a vertical lift. | Stack = vertical cross-section. Ocean floor → surface → solar lift → vapour. The 86%-from-oceans figure embeds in the bottom stratum. |
| 3 | **Phase Space** | Condensation as a 2D plot: altitude × temperature → where droplets form. | Phase Space's job is *show the territory, not the journey*. Cloud formation is a physical region in altitude-temp space — the pattern is literal. |
| 4 | **Versus** | Precipitation & collection — runoff vs infiltration. | The split happens at the soil. Versus's diagonal seam dramatises the bifurcation: surface flow on one side, groundwater on the other. |
| 5 | **Loop** | Close the cycle — name the four stations, mark the 2.5% freshwater stake with the `shu` seal. | The whole topic is a loop. Withholding the explicit loop until the close means the audience earns it. The freshwater stat lands as the seal, not a footnote. |

### Patterns deliberately *not* used

- **Field Note** — would need a real-world quote we don't have. No fabrication.
- **Versus** is used once (slide 4) — using it again for slide 1 (then/now climate) would dilute it.
- **Cliffhanger** — wrong tone. Awe, not suspense.
- **Surface** (bento grid) — would fragment a topic that is fundamentally one connected system.
- **Quadrant** — 2.5% freshwater could be a quadrant, but it's a single stake, not a 2x2.
- **Arc** — would be the obvious closer, but Loop is more honest: the cycle doesn't end.

## Anti-slop pre-flight

- ☐ No cyan-violet AI gradient anywhere.
- ☐ No KPI pill-card sea — numbers integrated into the slide, not floating in cards.
- ☐ No droplet mascots, no cartoon clouds.
- ☐ No "Did you know?" framing.
- ☐ One accent per slide. `shu` only on slide 5.
- ☐ At least 40% rice-paper ground per slide.
