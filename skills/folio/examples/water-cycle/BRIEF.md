# Brief · The Water Cycle

*Phase 1 of 4 · folio*

## 1. Audience

General/educational. Curious lay reader — could be a classroom, a museum panel, a public-science talk. Assumes no chemistry background. Wants the *story* of water more than the equations.

## 2. Anchor message

**Earth's water has been on the same loop for ~4 billion years. The drop in your glass has been a cloud, a river, a glacier, an ocean — and will be again.**

Everything else (evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection, scarcity) hangs off this single idea: *the same water, endlessly recycled.*

## 3. Tone

**Clear · visual · quietly awed.**

- One core idea per slide. No paragraph dumps.
- Numbers are anchors, not decoration: 4 billion years, 86% from oceans, 2.5% freshwater.
- Wonder, not whimsy. The cycle is a fact older than continents — let it be that.
- No marketing voice. No "unlock", no "empower", no "journey" as a verb.

## 4. Surface

Browser, full-screen, screen-share or projector. 16:9 viewport. Self-contained — no narrator notes required; the slides should hold up alone.

## 5. Off-limits

- Dense text blocks. If a slide needs a paragraph, it's the wrong slide.
- Stock cyan-violet AI gradient. The whole subject is *water and earth* — borrowed AI-deck colour would be a tell.
- Generic stat-card sea: avoid round-pill KPIs floating on dark.
- Cute droplet mascots, anthropomorphised clouds, cartoon suns.
- "Did you know?" framing. Trust the audience.
