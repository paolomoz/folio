# Anti-slop reference

Why folio refuses certain defaults.

## What AI slop in decks looks like

You can spot it from across the room:

- **Cyan-violet gradient** on every surface. The default LLM "tech" palette.
- **Round numbers** ŌĆö 95%, 73%, 42% ŌĆö sitting in pill cards.
- **Generic role names** ŌĆö primary, secondary, accent, success, warning, danger.
- **No ugly colour anywhere**. Every hue obediently harmonised.
- **No source story** ŌĆö colours arrived from a "harmony generator".
- **Universal applicability** ŌĆö the same palette could be a SaaS app, a wellness brand, or a fintech.
- **Glass-morphism + drop-shadow** stacking endlessly.
- **Slide patterns picked for visual appeal**, not job. Bento grids of unrelated content. Quadrants with arbitrary axes.
- **Type at 12px** because "it's elegant". It isn't ŌĆö it's invisible.
- **Borders at 0.5px** because "they're refined". They aren't ŌĆö they vanish on projectors.

## The antidote

Constrain by **sourcing**, not by harmony.

Every palette must:

1. **Cite a real-world reference.** Olivetti house style. The Casio F-91W LCD photographed at noon. A risograph zine. Each reference is specific enough to be Google-able.
2. **Include one deliberately wrong colour.** Telex's `muddy` is too brown. Casio's `amber` only appears on backlight press. Shoji's `shu` is one vermilion seal mark. Not "harmonised" ŌĆö punctuating.
3. **Use idiosyncratic role names.** `paper`, `ink`, `stamp`, `panel`, `digit`, `sodium`, `wine`. Names tell you what world the palette comes from.
4. **Declare forbidden pairings.** Kodachrome's "vermillion never on velvia". Shoji's "sumi for type, timber for structure ŌĆö they do not mix". Rules are enforced, not advisory.
5. **Refuse to be universal.** `use_for` and `avoid_for` are stated. Telex is for editorial readouts, not consumer marketing. Riso is for zines, not financial reports.

## The diagnostic

Before shipping a deck, ask:

- Could this palette have come from a different source than the one I picked? If yes, sourcing is too thin.
- Is there a colour I'm tempted to "fix" because it doesn't quite match? That's the wrong colour. Leave it.
- Are the role names interchangeable with another palette's? If yes, rename them to the world.
- Is the deck readable from 1m away on a projector? If anything is under 0.95rem or 2px, fix it.
- Does the slide pattern have a clear job, or did I pick it because it "looks nice"? If the latter, repick.
- If you removed all colour and animation, does the deck still say something? If not, the message isn't there yet.

## Slide-level rules

- **One accent per slide.** Borrowed from typography. The eye lands once, then reads.
- **No mystery-meat metaphors.** Every pattern has a stated job. Versus = then/now contrast. Quadrant = position-vs-position. Loop = cyclical process. Don't pick by visual appeal.
- **No data without a reading.** Charts, KPIs, tables must have a one-line interpretation visible. Numbers without a sentence are decoration.
- **The wrong colour appears once per slide**, not per deck. It's punctuation.
- **Type at screen-share scale.** Nothing under 0.95rem. Nothing under 2px borders.

## Why this matters

A deck that says one thing well beats a deck that says ten things blandly. AI defaults toward blandness because blandness is the average of all training data. Folio fights the average by sourcing every choice from somewhere specific ŌĆö and refusing to file off the rough edges that make the source recognisable.
